## What is Compounding?

Compounding is earning returns on your returns. When your ₹1,000 earns ₹120 in year one, in year two you earn returns on ₹1,120 — not just the original ₹1,000. Over time, this snowball effect creates exponential growth.

Albert Einstein reportedly called it the "eighth wonder of the world."

## The Tale of Two Investors

**Priya** starts a ₹10,000 monthly SIP at age 25. **Rahul** starts the same SIP at age 30. Both earn 12% annual returns and invest until age 55.

- **Priya** (30 years): Invests ₹36 lakh → Grows to **₹3.5 crore**
- **Rahul** (25 years): Invests ₹30 lakh → Grows to **₹1.9 crore**

Priya invested just ₹6 lakh more but ended up with ₹1.6 crore more. That's the power of 5 extra years of compounding.

## The Rule of 72

A quick way to estimate how long it takes to double your money: divide 72 by your annual return rate.

- At 12% returns: money doubles every **6 years**
- At 8% returns: money doubles every **9 years**
- At 6% (FD): money doubles every **12 years**

## Three Compounding Boosters

1. **Start early** — even small amounts grow massively over decades
2. **Stay invested** — don't interrupt compounding by withdrawing early
3. **Step up annually** — increasing your SIP by 10% each year supercharges growth

## The Cost of Waiting

Every year you delay costs you disproportionately. A 25-year-old needs ₹5,000/month to reach ₹1 crore by 55. A 35-year-old needs ₹16,000/month for the same goal. Time is your greatest asset.

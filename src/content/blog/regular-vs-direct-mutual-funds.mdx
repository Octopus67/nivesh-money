## The Basic Difference

Every mutual fund scheme has two plans: **Regular** and **Direct**.

- **Direct plan**: You invest directly with the AMC. Lower expense ratio (by 0.5–1%).
- **Regular plan**: You invest through a distributor/advisor. Slightly higher expense ratio, but you get professional guidance.

## The Expense Ratio Gap

A typical large-cap fund might charge:
- Direct plan: 0.5% expense ratio
- Regular plan: 1.0% expense ratio

Over 20 years on a ₹10,000 monthly SIP at 12% returns, the direct plan gives you roughly ₹8–10 lakh more. Sounds significant — but there's more to the story.

## The Value of Expert Guidance

What a good advisor provides:
- **Fund selection** — choosing from 2,000+ schemes based on your goals
- **Asset allocation** — the right mix of equity, debt, and hybrid
- **Rebalancing** — adjusting your portfolio as markets and life change
- **Behavioural coaching** — stopping you from panic-selling in crashes
- **Tax planning** — optimizing for LTCG, STCG, and indexation benefits

Studies show that investors with advisors earn 2–3% higher returns annually due to better behaviour and fund selection — far exceeding the 0.5% expense difference.

## When Direct Makes Sense

Direct plans work well if you:
- Have deep knowledge of mutual fund analysis
- Can commit time to regular portfolio review
- Won't make emotional decisions during market volatility

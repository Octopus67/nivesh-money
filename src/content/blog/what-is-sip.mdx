## What is a SIP?

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. Think of it as a recurring deposit, but instead of a bank, your money goes into market-linked instruments.

## How Does SIP Work?

When you start a SIP:
1. A fixed amount is debited from your bank account monthly
2. Mutual fund units are purchased at the current NAV
3. Over time, you accumulate units at different price points

This is called **rupee cost averaging** — you buy more units when prices are low and fewer when prices are high.

## The Power of Compounding

A ₹10,000 monthly SIP at 12% annual returns grows to:
- **₹23.2 lakh** in 10 years (invested: ₹12 lakh)
- **₹1 crore** in 20 years (invested: ₹24 lakh)
- **₹3.5 crore** in 30 years (invested: ₹36 lakh)

## Who Should Start a SIP?

SIPs are ideal for:
- Salaried professionals who can invest monthly
- First-time investors who want to start small
- Anyone with a long-term financial goal

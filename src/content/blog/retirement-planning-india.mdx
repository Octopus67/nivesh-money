## Why Start Early?

Every year you delay retirement planning costs you significantly. Inflation at 6% means your ₹50,000 monthly expenses today become ₹1.6 lakh in 20 years. The earlier you start, the less you need to invest each month.

## How Much Do You Need?

A simple rule of thumb: you need **25–30 times your annual expenses** at retirement. If you spend ₹6 lakh per year today and plan to retire in 20 years, accounting for inflation you'll need approximately ₹2.5 crore.

[Use our Retirement Calculator](/calculators/retirement) to find your exact number.

## The SIP Strategy

Starting a SIP early makes the math work in your favour:
- **Age 25, retiring at 60**: ₹8,000/month at 12% returns = ₹2.5 crore
- **Age 35, retiring at 60**: ₹25,000/month at 12% returns = ₹2.5 crore
- **Age 40, retiring at 60**: ₹50,000/month at 12% returns = ₹2.5 crore

A 10-year delay triples your required monthly investment.

## Building Your Retirement Plan

1. **Calculate your retirement corpus** using our [calculator](/calculators/retirement)
2. **Start a SIP** — even ₹5,000/month is a great beginning
3. **Increase annually** — a 10% step-up SIP dramatically boosts your corpus
4. **Diversify** across equity, debt, and hybrid funds based on your age
5. **Review yearly** and adjust for life changes

## Don't Forget Inflation

The biggest retirement planning mistake is ignoring inflation. ₹1 crore today will have the purchasing power of just ₹31 lakh in 20 years at 6% inflation.

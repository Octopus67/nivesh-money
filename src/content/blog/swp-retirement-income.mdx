## What is a SWP?

A Systematic Withdrawal Plan (SWP) lets you withdraw a fixed amount from your mutual fund investment at regular intervals. It's the reverse of a SIP — instead of putting money in, you take money out while the remaining corpus continues to grow.

## How Does SWP Work?

1. You invest a lump sum in a mutual fund (e.g., ₹50 lakh)
2. You set a monthly withdrawal amount (e.g., ₹25,000)
3. Each month, the fund redeems units worth ₹25,000 and credits your bank
4. The remaining investment continues earning returns

## Why SWP Over Fixed Deposits?

With SWP you get higher returns (8–12% vs 6–7% FD), better tax efficiency since only gains are taxed, full flexibility to change amounts, and built-in inflation protection through market-linked growth.

## Example: ₹50 Lakh Corpus

With a ₹50 lakh corpus at 10% annual returns and ₹30,000 monthly withdrawal:
- Your corpus lasts **25+ years**
- Total withdrawn: **₹90 lakh**
- That's nearly double your initial investment
